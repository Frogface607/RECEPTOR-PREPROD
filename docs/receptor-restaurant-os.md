# Receptor Restaurant OS

Receptor is a SaaS operating system for restaurants. It combines live operating
data, venue context, AI assistance, menu workflows, team tasks and integrations
in one workspace.

## Positioning

Receptor is not a generic chatbot wrapper. The product starts by building a
structured memory of the restaurant, then uses that context together with iiko
data and operational modules.

Core promise:

- see what is happening in the restaurant;
- understand what requires attention;
- assign the next action to the right role;
- keep the operating rhythm repeatable.

## Product Surfaces

### Owner Cockpit

Owner-level dashboard for daily control:

- Morning Brief;
- revenue, checks, dishes and shifts;
- risks and opportunities;
- weekly management report;
- AI answers grounded in venue context.

### Context Engine

Structured restaurant profile:

- venue identity and format;
- owner goals;
- economics and constraints;
- team and roles;
- systems and integrations;
- AI/data policy.

### Menu OS

Menu management layer:

- QR menu;
- stop-list;
- tech cards;
- food cost;
- dish launch materials;
- iiko article mapping.

### Team OS

Operational layer for staff:

- memberships and roles;
- task assignment by member, role or venue;
- task comments;
- announcements;
- training and knowledge-base direction.

### Integration Pack

Integration layer:

- iiko Cloud/RMS;
- future R-Keeper support;
- Telegram/VK channels;
- configurable AI providers.

## Commercial Model

Receptor is sold as a modular subscription:

- Starter: one venue, core cockpit and context;
- Pro OS: owner cockpit, Menu OS, Team OS and integrations;
- Group: multi-venue setup, custom roles and separate data policy.

One-time setup may be sold separately when a customer needs data connection,
context collection, team onboarding and module configuration.

## Implementation Principle

Every feature should answer one of these questions:

- What changed in the restaurant?
- Why does it matter?
- Who should act?
- What should be done next?

Internal launch plans and customer-specific negotiations should not appear in
the product UI. Specific restaurant deployments are implementation cases, not
the product itself.
