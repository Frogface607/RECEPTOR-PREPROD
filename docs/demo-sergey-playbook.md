# Receptor Pilot Demo Playbook

Date: 2026-06-11

## Goal

Do not demo "a website" or "27 AI tools".

Demo a pilot scenario:

> Receptor is an AI operating system above iiko. It understands the restaurant,
> reads business data, lowers human-factor risk, and helps the owner / brand chef
> make daily decisions without drowning in Excel, chats, and back-office reports.

The ask:

> Give me one real iiko Cloud API key and one week of feedback. I will configure
> Receptor around your restaurant and show whether it saves time, increases
> visibility, and reduces operational chaos.

## Core Story

Tell it from lived pain:

> My problem was not that there were no reports. Reports existed. The problem was
> that there was no system. I had high ratings and full rooms, but still too much
> depended on people, memory, chats, and manual control. Receptor is the system I
> wished I had: it sees the restaurant as a whole, knows the context, reads iiko,
> and helps decide what to do next.

## Demo Flow

### 1. Context: Why Receptor Exists

Say:

> iiko remains the source of truth. I am not replacing it. Receptor sits above it:
> it reads data, understands context, and turns it into decisions and tasks.

Keep it short. Do not start with features.

### 2. Venue Profile / Deep Research

Show first if research works well.

Message:

> Before Copilot answers, it needs to understand the restaurant: format, concept,
> audience, reviews, strengths, weak spots, operating risks, and owner goals.

What should be visible:

- venue name;
- city;
- type;
- owner context;
- "research / fill profile" action;
- strengths;
- guest pains;
- operating risks;
- recommended focus.

Important:

- If sources are not shown, call it "profile draft", not proven research.
- Separate facts from assumptions.
- If research is weak, do not linger here.

### 3. iiko Connection

Message:

> With an active Cloud API key, we select the organization and start reading
> live data. Receptor is read-first: it does not break the back office.

Ask:

> Can we create a separate technical user / integration key for the pilot?

If key is unavailable:

> No problem. Then I take this as setup homework and we pilot once your admin
> gives the key. The value is not in the key creation, it is in what we do with
> the data after.

### 4. BI / Owner Cockpit

Show only as "example cockpit" if data is demo.

Message:

> This screen answers: what happened, where money moved, what categories and
> dishes matter, and what deserves attention today.

Do not oversell charts. Focus on decisions:

- what changed;
- what is risky;
- what to inspect today;
- what to ask Copilot.

### 5. Copilot

This is the heart.

Ask questions like:

- "What should I check today as a brand chef?"
- "Where can human factor cost us money this week?"
- "What menu/category risks should I inspect first?"
- "What would you do this week if you were responsible for this restaurant?"

Message:

> The important part is not chat. The important part is that the chat knows the
> restaurant profile and uses live iiko data.

### 6. Tech Card Studio

Show as second wow, not the main pitch.

Message:

> Receptor also turns decisions into operating documents. For a dish, it can
> create a tech-card draft, food cost, KBJU, preflight, PDF/JSON, and materials
> for the waiters.

Show:

- AI draft;
- quality preflight;
- PDF / JSON;
- Launch Pack.

Do not spend too long in tables.

## Show / No-Show

### Show

- Venue profile.
- iiko connection screen, if explained calmly.
- Dashboard only with clear "example vs live" framing.
- Copilot.
- Tech Card Studio as proof of operating system direction.

### Avoid Showing

- generic tools grid as the main product;
- anything that says or feels like "27 prompts";
- fake/mock dish names as if they are real;
- pages with obvious layout issues on tablet;
- deep research results if they look invented;
- long pricing page;
- internal roadmap docs.

## What Can Trigger "Another AI Toy" Reaction

- too many buttons;
- generic ChatGPT-style advice;
- no source/fact distinction;
- mock data presented as live;
- flashy language without a specific business action;
- "AI tools" before "restaurant operating system";
- tables that look like a calculator, not a workflow;
- you apologizing for unfinished parts.

## Risk Handling

### If iiko key does not work

Say:

> Then today is not the data pilot yet. Today I am showing the operating layer.
> Give me a technical integration key, and I will configure the first live pilot.

### If deep research is weak

Say:

> This is the profile draft. The stronger version combines web research, your own
> corrections, and real iiko data.

### If dashboard uses demo data

Say:

> This is the cockpit shape. With your Cloud API key, these become your real
> categories, dishes, checks, and periods.

### If asked "How is it different from iiko?"

Say:

> iiko records operations. Receptor explains operations and helps decide what to
> do next. It is the AI operating layer above the back office.

## Pilot Ask

Ask for:

- active iiko Cloud API key;
- separate technical user/integration if possible;
- one venue / one organization first;
- one Telegram/contact channel for feedback;
- one week of real use;
- 2-3 concrete business questions from Sergey.

Suggested ask:

> Let me pilot this on one restaurant for a week. I will configure the profile,
> connect iiko, tune the dashboard and Copilot around your real questions. At the
> end of the week we decide if it is useful and what it should become for the
> holding.

## One-Minute Pitch

> Receptor is an AI operating system for restaurants. It sits above iiko, learns
> the restaurant context, reads live data, and helps the owner or brand chef see
> what is happening and what to do next. My pain was that a restaurant can have
> guests, ratings, and revenue, but still lack system: too much depends on people,
> memory, chats, and manual reports. Receptor lowers that human factor. It gives
> BI, Copilot, tech cards, menu work, and team materials in one operating layer.
> I want to pilot it on one of your restaurants for a week and build it around
> your real questions.

## Questions To Ask Sergey

- What do you check first when you look at a restaurant's performance?
- Which reports do you trust today?
- Where do managers waste your time?
- What is hardest to control across venues?
- What do you wish iiko explained better?
- What would you ask a perfect restaurant analyst every morning?
- What is one decision you would like Receptor to help with this week?

## Cleanup Before Demo

Critical:

- Replace obvious fake/demo dish names in visible hero/demo screens.
- Make deep research honest: "profile draft" unless sources are available.
- Do not present demo data as live.
- Keep the demo route short and controlled.
- Test on tablet before leaving.

Nice to have:

- calmer hero copy;
- less "AI tools" language;
- larger Copilot surface;
- fewer badges;
- clear "live data after iiko key" labels.
